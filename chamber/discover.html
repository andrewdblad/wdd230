Discover Boise Idaho

Demographics

Boise, Idaho, is a vibrant and diverse city with a growing population:

  • Population: Approximately 250,000 (2021)
  • Education: A highly educated community with a significant portion holding bachelor's degrees or higher.
  • Employment: A thriving job market with opportunities in technology, healthcare, education, and outdoor industries.
  • Cultural Diversity: Boise embraces a rich cultural tapestry, with residents from various backgrounds contributing to its vibrant atmosphere.

    • Area Features

    Boise is known for its exceptional quality of life and a wide range of attractions and amenities:

  • Outdoor Recreation: The city offers easy access to outdoor adventures with nearby hiking trails, biking paths, and world-class skiing in the nearby mountains.
  • Boise River: The Boise River Greenbelt provides a scenic route for walking, running, and biking, winding through the heart of the city.
  • Cultural Scene: Boise boasts a thriving arts and cultural community, with theaters, galleries, and music venues offering diverse entertainment options.
  • Downtown: The downtown area features a vibrant dining scene, boutique shopping, and a lively nightlife.
  • Boise State University: Home to Boise State Broncos, the university adds a youthful energy to the city and offers various cultural and sporting events.
  • Boise's Food Scene: Known for its farm-to-table dining options, Boise offers a diverse culinary experience.
  • Historical Sites: Explore historical sites like the Old Idaho Penitentiary, the Basque Block, and the Idaho State Capitol.
  • Boise's Green Initiatives: The city is committed to sustainability, with numerous green initiatives and eco-friendly practices.

    • Statistics

    Boise's continued growth and economic stability make it an attractive destination for businesses and residents:

  • Unemployment Rate: Historically lower than the national average.
  • Business-Friendly Environment: Boise is recognized as a top city for entrepreneurs and startups.
  • Housing Market: A stable housing market with diverse housing options to accommodate various lifestyles.
  • Education: Strong educational institutions, including Boise State University.
  • Healthcare: Access to high-quality healthcare facilities and medical services.
